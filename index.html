My Cosplay Collection:

This is the remake of my Mirror Mirror Snow White cosplay, and is not based on the Disney Film. I remade this costume mostly because I knew I could make it far better and it's still one of my favourite costumes in a live action film. It took me about 14 months from start to finish. I stated working on it in January of 2021 and finished in February of 2022. This costume was made from the bottom up, I started with the base undergarments and worked my way up to the gown, then accessories.

My first ever crossplay- this costume was made during my spare time during my first semester at Fanshawe College's Costume Production program, intended for debut at Ottawa Comic Con 2019, but only debuted in the fall of 2019. I started by altering some commercial patterns by McCalls, a turtle neck sweater and sweatpants, into the right shapes for Shin-ah's base outfit.

This costume was made during the Spring and Summer of 2022 alongside my friend Mae-Gwyn Cosplay's Homura cosplay as part of our World Cosplay Summit Canada Preliminary set. These are the costumes we won the preliminary with.

This costume was made as a second piece for the Scrap Epic Challenge Quarantine Edition as issued by Kirilee Cosplay and Alana Owlette over on youtube. The challenge includes using only materials you have on hand, so I decided that what I had in combination with patterns and wigs I had on hand to make an original Princess Zelda costume.

Meet the Team: Annie

I'm a cosplayer and designer originally from Glengarry county, Ontario, and currently in Brockville, Ontario. I have been a cosplayer since 2010, and an aspiring fashion and costume designer since 2014. I have had a passion for video games, movies, anime and nerd culture since I was very young, with my earliest costuming adventures being when I was 6 years old dressing up as Sailor Mercury. Now I have been cosplaying for over a decade and make all my own pieces. Cosplaying led to fascination with the construction and design of all sorts of clothing which in hand led me to study Fashion Design at Fanshawe College in London Ontario, which I graduated from in 2018. The following year, in 2019, I graduated from Fanshawe's Costume Production Post Graduate program, after completing a Work Placement at Upper Canada Village. I enjoy historical, theatrical and, of course, hobby costuming and currently work at Bonita Bold Fashion in Brockville Ontario as a seamstress and pattern maker. I often compete in ICG Masquerades at events, and have been an ICG Master Level Cosplayer since 2016. On August 7th, 2022, myself and Mae-Gwyn cosplay, as Team AnniMae, won the World Cosplay Summit Canadian Preliminary, making us Team Canada for 2023. We will be traveling to Nagoya, Japan in the summer of 2023 to represent the Canadian Cosplay Community alongside representatives from all over the world.
